OVERVIEW

THE PROBLEMS

Our world is becoming more digital by the day, and with that, millions of people have growing data storage needs. While the current cloud storage market is highly saturated, a few problems exist with existing cloud services:

  • Hard-to-use organization
  • Outdated interfaces
  • Inability to upload and organize web content
  • Expensive storage

THE SOLUTIONS

I employed an iterative design approach throughout the entire project. By consistently testing my designs, I was able to hone in on an MVP that solved specific problems for cloud storage users:

  • Create an intuitive organization system that prioritizes easy uploads and file previews
  • Design a sleek, modern interface
  • Allow users to organize web content organization
  • Provide ample free storage
DISCOVERY AND RESEARCH

USER SURVEY

While I made some assumptions on which features would improve existing cloud services, I needed to survey real users to better understand their cloud storage needs.

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

In order to better understand the competition, I needed to analyze the strengths and weaknesses of some of the big names in cloud storage. Google Drive, DropBox and iCloud were the most used cloud services in my survey group.

GOOGLE DRIVE SWOT ANALYSIS

STRENGTHS

  • Clean, easy-to-navigate interface
  • Strong content creation
  • Integration across all Google Apps

WEAKNESSES

  • Security/privacy concerns
  • Some competitors offer more free storage
  • Unable to organize web content

OPPORTUNITIES

  • Rapidly growing demand for data storage
  • Many people switching to cloud storage

THREATS

  • Rapidly growing competition
  • Many users have concerns with data safety

After perfoming a SWOT analysis on these three competitors, I found that a new cloud service might succeed in the market by drawing heavily from Google Drive’s design language while creating an intuitive design system, further modernizing the interface, enabling web content organization and offering more free storage.

BRANDING

THE BRAND

The majority of my surveyed users were under 30 years old, so I wanted to design a brand that felt sleek and modern. To differentiate my brand from the competition, I integrated clean shapes and new colors into my designs.

canopy was chosen as the app’s name for a number of reasons. Canopies mark the highest point of a forest, often residing amongst the clouds. They nurture, enclose and protect an entire ecosystem, keeping the forest’s life safe and secure. Similar to a canopy, a well designed cloud service should nurture and protect users’ data. Many trees link up to form a canopy, just as many files link up to form a cloud drive.

LOGO DESIGN

Brainstorming
Digital

Canopy’s logo was created by arranging three circles, three triangles and one rectangle into a cloud shape. Triangles attached to circles at either end create two leaves, representing the leaves of a canopy. The logo sits above the text, much like a canopy exists at the top of the forest.

COLOR PALETTE

I went with a warm, monochromatic green color scheme to stand out from the (mostly blue) competition.

TYPOGRAPHY

Poppins is a sans serif geometric typeface that creates a modern yet warm atmosphere. Open Sans complements Poppins well as it is very readable and renders quite nicely on the browser.

VISUAL DESIGN

HI-FIDELITY MOCKUPS

I coupled my wireframes and branding to create hi-fi mockups. Some features included:

  • Modern homepage with a dark green background
  • Illustrations to add a warm, human element
  • Added product features, storage plans and testimonials to homepage
  • Sleek, simple log-in/sign-up screens
  • Dark sidebar on dashboard to provide contrast bewteen tab navigation and content
  • Large file previews to prioritize content, especially photos
