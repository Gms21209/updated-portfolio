OVERVIEW

THE PROBLEMS

Our world is becoming more digital by the day, and with that, millions of people have growing data storage needs. While the current cloud storage market is highly saturated, a few problems exist with existing cloud services:

  • Hard-to-use organization
  • Outdated interfaces
  • Inability to upload and organize web content
  • Expensive storage

THE SOLUTIONS

I employed an iterative design approach throughout the entire project. By consistently testing my designs, I was able to hone in on an MVP that solved specific problems for cloud storage users:

  • Create an intuitive organization system that prioritizes easy uploads and file previews
  • Design a sleek, modern interface
  • Allow users to organize web content organization
  • Provide ample free storage
DISCOVERY AND RESEARCH

USER SURVEY

While I made some assumptions on which features would improve existing cloud services, I needed to survey real users to better understand their cloud storage needs.

KEY FINDINGS

86%

of those surveyed use Google Drive, followed by 58% for iCloud and 41% for DropBox.

92%

most often use their cloud storage service on a desktop computer.

92%

store photos on cloud services, followed by 75% for text-based documents and 71% for videos.

79%

use cloud services for file storage, followed by 71% for file organization and 67 percent for file sharing.

54%

use their cloud storage service to organize web content.

63%

pay for extra storage, but 67% pay less than $5/month.

USER PERSONAS

JOSH

22-year-old college student from Austin, TX

BIO

Josh is a senior in college. He is currently enrolled in five classes, a number of which require him to work on group projects with his classmates. Independent of schoolwork, Josh is an avid internet surfer. He has spent most of his spare change on late night meals, so he is looking to save money wherever he can.

GOALS

  • Organize files in a smart, intuitive way using folders
  • Store data in a safe, organized place
  • Organize web content
  • Share files with classmates

FRUSTRATIONS

  • Interface is not clean, modern or intuitive
  • Current services do not offer ample free storage

SOPHIA

26-year-old photographer from Dallas, TX

BIO

Sophia is a freelance photographer who has amassed a sizeable following on social media. She works with a number of different clients at a single time, so keeping her photo files organized is a necessity. She has recently ventured into videography, so she is looking for a cloud service that can accommodate large files.

GOALS

  • Store photos and videos in a centralized location
  • Organize photos and videos into folders
  • Share photos and videos
  • Find a cloud service offering more free storage

FRUSTRATIONS

  • It is hard to locate specific photos on cloud drive
  • Feels she is paying too much for storage

COMPETITIVE ANALYSIS

In order to better understand the competition, I needed to analyze the strengths and weaknesses of some of the big names in cloud storage. Google Drive, DropBox and iCloud were the most used cloud services in my survey group.

GOOGLE DRIVE SWOT ANALYSIS

STRENGTHS

  • Clean, easy-to-navigate interface
  • Strong content creation
  • Integration across all Google Apps

WEAKNESSES

  • Security/privacy concerns
  • Some competitors offer more free storage
  • Unable to organize web content

OPPORTUNITIES

  • Rapidly growing demand for data storage
  • Many people switching to cloud storage

THREATS

  • Rapidly growing competition
  • Many users have concerns with data safety

After perfoming a SWOT analysis on these three competitors, I found that a new cloud service might succeed in the market by drawing heavily from Google Driveâ€™s design language while creating an intuitive design system, further modernizing the interface, enabling web content organization and offering more free storage.
